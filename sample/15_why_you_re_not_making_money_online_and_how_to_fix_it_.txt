WHY YOU’RE NOT MAKING MONEY ONLINE (AND HOW TO FIX IT)

I’ve lost more money online than most people make in a year. I’ve chased shiny objects, trusted the wrong people, and wasted years on strategies that sounded good but never paid. Here are five brutal lessons I learned the hard way—so you don’t have to.

---

FALLING FOR "GET RICH QUICK" PROMISES KILLS YOUR BANK ACCOUNT

I bought a $997 course that promised I’d make $10,000 in 30 days with "zero effort." The sales page had fake screenshots, fake testimonials, and a fake countdown timer. I ignored the red flags because I wanted to believe it. Three months later, I was out a grand, my credit card was maxed, and I had nothing to show for it except a folder full of useless PDFs.

The cost? Financial: $1,000 gone. Emotional: Shame, frustration, and a month of wasted energy. The worst part? I knew better. I just let greed override common sense.

RULE: If a program guarantees fast money with no work, it’s a scam. Real online income takes time. Assume any offer that sounds too good to be true is a lie. Research the creator—if they don’t have a real business outside of selling courses, walk away.

---

CHASING EVERY NEW PLATFORM LEAVES YOU BROKE AND BURNT OUT

I jumped from dropshipping to crypto to NFTs to AI tools—whatever the gurus were hyping that month. I’d spend weeks learning a new platform, set up a store or a funnel, then abandon it when the next trend hit. I never stuck around long enough to see results.

The cost? Financial: $3,500 in tools, ads, and courses I never used. Emotional: Constant overwhelm, self-doubt, and the feeling that I’d never "get it." I was always starting over instead of building something real.

RULE: Pick one proven method and stick with it for at least 6 months. Master it before moving on. If you’re not seeing progress after 90 days, adjust—but don’t quit. Most people fail because they quit too soon, not because the method doesn’t work.

---

IGNORING TRAFFIC MEANS YOU’LL NEVER MAKE A DIME

I built a perfect affiliate site—great content, solid SEO, beautiful design. I waited for the money to roll in. Six months later, I had 50 visitors a month and $0 in commissions. I assumed "if I build it, they will come." Wrong.

The cost? Financial: $1,200 in hosting, plugins, and outsourcing. Emotional: Crushing disappointment when the site flopped. I wasted time on perfection instead of what actually matters: getting eyeballs on my offer.

RULE: Traffic is oxygen. If you don’t have a plan to get visitors—SEO, paid ads, email lists, social media—you don’t have a business. Start driving traffic on day one, even if your site isn’t "perfect." No traffic = no sales.

---

SELLING BEFORE SERVING TANKS YOUR REPUTATION

I launched a coaching program before I had any real results. I cobbled together a curriculum from free YouTube videos, slapped a $500 price tag on it, and promoted it hard. The first client asked for a refund after one session. The second left a scathing review. My credibility was shot before I even got started.

The cost? Financial: $200 in refunds. Emotional: Embarrassment, lost confidence, and a damaged reputation. I had to rebuild trust from scratch.

RULE: Only sell what you’ve already done successfully. If you’re teaching others how to make money online, you better have made money online first. Get results for yourself, then package that knowledge and sell it. Anything else is exploitation.

---

WAITING FOR "PERFECT" MEANS YOU’LL NEVER START

I spent a year planning my first online business. I bought domain names, designed logos, wrote business plans, and researched endlessly. I never launched. The market changed, my ideas became outdated, and I was back at square one.

The cost? Financial: $800 in tools and courses I never used. Emotional: Regret, frustration, and the realization that I’d wasted a year of my life. The perfect time to start never comes.

RULE: Launch fast, iterate faster. Your first version will suck—and that’s okay. Get something out there, get feedback, and improve. The only real failure is not starting.

---

STOP LEARNING, START DOING

I’ve made every mistake in the book. The difference between me now and me then? I stopped consuming and started creating. I stopped chasing and started building. You don’t need another course, another webinar, or another guru. You need to pick a method, take action, and stick with it long enough to see results.

The money is out there—but it won’t come to you. Go get it.
