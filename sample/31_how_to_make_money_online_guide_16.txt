how to make money online? — Guide #16

THE MYTH OF PASSIVE INCOME

You see the ads everywhere. "Quit your 9-to-5 in 30 days!" "Earn $10,000 a month while you sleep!" They sell you the dream of passive income like it’s a golden ticket. The reality? Passive income is a myth until you’ve bled for it. Every dollar that rolls in without your active effort was paid for in advance—with sweat, sleepless nights, and relentless problem-solving.

Take affiliate marketing. You think it’s about slapping links on a blog and watching the commissions roll in. Wrong. First, you spend months writing content that no one reads. Then you learn SEO, backlink strategies, and how to game algorithms that change every three months. You burn through $500 on courses from gurus who promise "secret hacks" but only teach what you could’ve found on a free YouTube tutorial. By month six, you’ve made $87. The gurus? They’re driving Lamborghinis—off the backs of people like you who bought the dream.

THE GRIND BEHIND THE SCREENS

You want to know how people really make money online? They grind. Hard. Not the "work four hours a week" grind. The "I haven’t left my apartment in three days and my diet consists of cold pizza and energy drinks" grind.

Freelancers on Upwork or Fiverr don’t just wake up to $1,000 gigs. They start by undercutting everyone, charging $5 for work that takes five hours. They deal with clients who disappear after receiving the work, or demand endless revisions because "it’s not what I envisioned." They spend hours crafting proposals that get ignored. The ones who survive? They niche down. They specialize in something so specific—like "Shopify stores for vegan pet food brands"—that they become the only option. Then, and only then, do they raise their rates.

Dropshipping is another beast. You think it’s about picking a trending product and watching the sales pour in. The truth? You’ll spend $2,000 on Facebook ads before you see a single profit. You’ll deal with suppliers who send broken products, customers who demand refunds for "not liking the color," and chargebacks that eat into your margins. The winners? They treat it like a science. They split-test ad creatives, audience segments, and landing pages until they find the one combination that converts. Then they scale—until Facebook bans their account for "suspicious activity" and they have to start over.

THE DARK SIDE OF SCALING

Scaling is the holy grail. You’ve made your first $1,000 online? Great. Now make $10,000. But scaling isn’t just about doing more of what works. It’s about managing chaos.

Take course creators. They film a $2,000 course, launch it, and make $50,000 in a week. Then the real work begins. Customers flood their inbox with questions. "How do I download the videos?" "This module isn’t loading." "I paid for lifetime access, why is it locked?" They hire a virtual assistant for $5 an hour to handle support. The VA quits after a week because the job is soul-crushing. They hire another. This one steals their email list and starts selling their own knockoff course.

Or consider SaaS founders. They build a tool that solves a problem, get 100 paying users, and think they’ve made it. Then the server crashes during peak hours. Customers rage. They scramble to fix it, but the damage is done. Churn spikes. They spend $10,000 on a DevOps engineer to prevent it from happening again. The engineer leaves after a month. The cycle repeats.

THE HIDDEN COSTS NO ONE TALKS ABOUT

You think making money online is cheap? Think again. The hidden costs will bleed you dry before you see a profit.

First, there’s the tech. You need a domain ($15/year), hosting ($30/month), a premium theme ($200), and tools like Ahrefs ($99/month) or ClickFunnels ($297/month). Then there’s the ads. Facebook, Google, TikTok—each platform will happily take your money while showing your ads to bots and uninterested users. You’ll burn $1,000 before you get a single sale.

Then there’s the time. Time is the real currency. You’ll spend hours learning skills that become obsolete in a year. You’ll chase trends that die before you can capitalize on them. You’ll pivot, restart, and pivot again. The opportunity cost is staggering. That time could’ve been spent climbing the corporate ladder, earning a steady paycheck, and actually having a life.

THE PSYCHOLOGICAL TOLL

The worst part? The isolation. No one warns you about the loneliness of making money online. You work from home, surrounded by screens, with no coworkers to vent to. Your friends and family don’t get it. "Why don’t you just get a real job?" they ask. You try to explain, but they zone out after "affiliate marketing."

You’ll doubt yourself. Constantly. Every failed launch, every ignored email, every refund request chips away at your confidence. You’ll question if you’re cut out for this. The gurus won’t tell you that. They’ll show you their six-figure months and act like it’s effortless.

THE ONES WHO MAKE IT

So who actually makes it? The ones who treat it like a business, not a get-rich-quick scheme. They invest in mentors who’ve actually done it, not just sold courses. They build systems, not just income streams. They understand that overnight success takes years.

They also know when to quit. Not every idea will work. Not every niche is profitable. The winners aren’t the ones with the best ideas—they’re the ones who execute, adapt, and outlast the competition.

If you’re still reading this, you’re either naive or determined. If it’s the latter, welcome to the grind. It’s brutal, it’s relentless, and it’s not for everyone. But if you can stomach the chaos, the rewards are real. Just don’t expect them to come easy.
